A barangay is the smallest political unit in the country. Generally, one enumerator is assigned to
enumerate one barangay. For enumeration purposes, a large barangay is usually divided into parts, and
each part is called an enumeration area (EA)

Source of Water Supply*:
Record the source of water supply. Access to clean water is essential for the health and
well-being of the household. Write down the source of water supply for the household.
This can be as follows:
Level I - A protected well or a developed spring with an outlet but without a distribution
system, generally adaptable for rural areas where the houses are thinly scattered. A Level
I facility normally serves an average of 15 households.
Level II - A water supply facility composed of a source, a reservoir, a piped distribution
network with adequate treatment facility, and communal faucets. Usually, one faucet
serves 4 to 6 households. Generally suitable for rural and urban fringe areas where
houses are clustered densely to justify a simple piped system. The definition was modified
with the inclusion of the underlined phrase 'with adequate treatment' to emphasize that
source of water supply has passed the Philippine National Standards for Drinking Water
Department of Health, Philippines

Level III - A water supply facility with a source, a reservoir, a piped distribution
network with adequate treatment facility and household taps. Level III water supply
facility/service is generally suited for densely populated urban areas. The definition
was modified with the inclusion of the underlined phrase 'with adequate treatment' to
emphasize that the source of water supply has passed the Philippine National
Standards for Drinking Water.

Process in HH Profiling
Source: FHSIS MOP ver. 2018

It is expected that each HH in the catchment barangay has been assigned to a
specific BHW or other community volunteer worker. Ideally, each BHW should cover
about 20-25 HHs. However, this is not usually followed especially if there are only a
few BHWs in the barangay. Other volunteer workers or health staff (e.g.
NDPs/RHMPP”s) are also mobilized to handle specific HHs in a given barangay. As
a process, the HH profiling entails the following steps:
1. HH profiling must be conducted and completed at the beginning of the year
(January), and must be updated on a quarterly basis. HH Profiles must be
completed/ updated at the end of the first month of each quarter;

Department of Health, Philippines

Process in HH Profiling
Source: FHSIS MOP ver. 2018

2. The designated BHW/community volunteer worker/NDP shall conduct the
profiling of HHs assigned to her by the midwife (MW). The MW must ensure that
all HHs in her catchment barangay has been assigned to a specific
BHW/community volunteer worker/NDP;
3. All accomplished HH profiles will be submitted to their supervising midwives not
later than the third week of January (and the first months of every quarter for the
updated profiles); and
4. The MW supervisor will compile all completed HH profiles to become the basis
for master listing the different categories of clients to be reached/served.
Note: the deadline for 2023 HH profiling is on or before September 29, 2023 (DM No. 2023-0060-A), and this is a onetime submission to the DOH-Epidemiology Bureau. The updated 2023 quarterly data is not required to submit.
